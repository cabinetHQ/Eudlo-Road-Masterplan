# System Options Comparison

## Purpose

Compare practical energy system options before requesting final supplier quotes.

All costs are placeholders until commercial quotes are received.

| Option | Capital cost placeholder | Expected benefit | Pros | Cons | Best use case | Recommendation status |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Option A: 30 kW solar + no battery | TBD | Lower daytime grid imports | Lowest capital cost; simple install; useful if daytime workshop load is strong | No evening backup; limited outage resilience; may not support future growth; weaker bill reduction | First step if capital is tightly constrained and daytime load is confirmed | Not preferred for Stage 1 |
| Option B: 50 kW solar + 50 kWh battery | TBD | Good daytime reduction with limited storage | Lower battery cost than 100 kWh; better than solar-only; can support selected outage loads | Battery may be undersized for workshop and evening loads; may need early upgrade | Transitional option if quotes show 100 kWh battery is too expensive | Consider only if 100 kWh case is uneconomic |
| Option C: 50 kW solar + 100 kWh battery | TBD | Strong bill reduction, better resilience, practical Stage 1 base | Matches current Stage 1 target; supports future microgrid capability; useful battery depth | Higher upfront cost; still needs grid for reliability; quote quality matters | Preferred first serious business-case option | Preferred Stage 1 option |
| Option D: 100 kW solar + 100 kWh battery | TBD | Maximum near-term solar capacity and stronger daytime supply | Gets closer to future target immediately; better for future loads | Higher capital risk before interval data; possible export constraints; may be oversized for current load | Only if quotes, network rules, and load data justify immediate expansion | Defer pending data |
| Option E: staged 50 kW now, expand to 100 kW later | TBD | Balances near-term savings with future expansion | Reduces rework; limits initial capital; lets data guide Stage 2; fits financial decision approach | Requires careful design now; Stage 2 may cost more later; supplier must document expansion pathway | Best fit for uncertain load growth and staged property development | Recommended strategy |

## Quote Requirements

Every quote should show:

- Cash price
- Finance option if offered
- STCs shown separately
- Battery incentives shown separately
- Inverter capacity
- Battery inverter capacity
- Export control assumptions
- Generator integration approach
- 100 kW expansion pathway
- Exclusions and required electrical upgrades

## Working Recommendation

Use Option C as the Stage 1 technical model and Option E as the delivery strategy.

This means the project should initially price and assess 50 kW solar + 100 kWh battery, but design switchboards, cable pathways, inverter locations, monitoring, and layout so 100 kW solar remains practical later.
