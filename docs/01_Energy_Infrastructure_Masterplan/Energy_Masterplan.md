# Energy Infrastructure Masterplan

## Current Known Position

- Current known power bill: $1,200 per month average
- Estimated annual electricity cost: $14,400
- Known usage: approximately 93 kWh/day average
- Likely workday load is higher than the average because workshop activity is not evenly distributed across all days

## Stage 1 Target

Stage 1 should initially be modelled as:

- 50 kW solar
- 100 kWh battery
- Generator integration
- Electrical design that allows future expansion

## Future Expansion Target

The infrastructure should be designed for future expansion to:

- 100 kW solar
- Additional battery storage if load growth or tariff structure supports it
- Future dam pump load
- Future EV charging load
- Additional workshop and manufacturing loads

## Conservative Solar Assumption

For planning purposes, 100 kW of solar panels should be treated as a reliable 40-60 kW daytime source, not as 100 kW continuous output.

This assumption should be used when assessing daytime workshop operation, battery charging, EV charging, and future pump loads.

## Generator Integration

Generator integration should be considered part of the site energy architecture rather than a separate emergency-only add-on.

Planning questions:

- What critical circuits should the generator support?
- Should the generator charge the battery during extended outages?
- What manual or automatic transfer arrangements are needed?
- What generator capacity is required for workshop continuity?

## Future Loads To Allow For

### Dam Pump

The dam pump may become a significant intermittent load. The energy system should allow for pump scheduling during solar generation windows where practical.

### EV Charging

Future EV charging should be treated as a controllable load. Charging should preferably occur during solar generation windows or low-cost tariff periods.

### Workshop Load

Major machine loads need to be listed and profiled. Peak draw, start-up current, duty cycle, and typical operating hours should be captured before final system sizing.

## Open Questions

- What is the actual 15-minute or 30-minute interval load profile?
- What is the maximum site demand during busy workshop days?
- Which loads are flexible and can be shifted into solar windows?
- What tariff options are available for the site?
- What export limits apply?
- What battery inverter capacity is required, separate from battery kWh capacity?
