# Dam Solar Pump Concept

## Purpose

Develop a practical concept for a solar-only dam recirculation pump that improves water movement, supports a creek/cascade feature, and contributes to safety and erosion control around a lower plunge basin or waterhole.

This is a non-critical system. It does not need the same reliability standard as workshop power or fire safety systems.

## Concept Summary

| Item | Current concept |
| --- | ---: |
| Pump type | Solar-only recirculation pump |
| Flow target | Approximately 6,000 L/hour |
| Lift target | Approximately 20 m |
| Panel target | 3-5 kW depending on shade |
| Pump target | 1.1-1.5 kW |
| Return style | Creek/cascade preferred over fountain |
| Lower feature | Plunge basin or waterhole concept |
| Panel source | Second-hand panels acceptable |

## Solar-Only Pump Concept

The pump should run when solar is available and stop when solar is insufficient. Because the system is non-critical, it does not need battery backup unless later design work shows a specific need.

This approach keeps the dam pump separate from the main workshop battery system and avoids using stored energy for a non-critical daytime water feature.

## Second-Hand Panels

Second-hand panels are acceptable for this non-critical system if they are electrically safe, suitable for the pump controller, and installed in a compliant manner.

This may be a good use for lower-cost panels because the system can tolerate variable output and does not need guaranteed production.

## Creek/Cascade Preference

A creek or cascade return is preferred over a fountain because it may:

- Provide better visual integration with the land
- Improve aeration and water movement
- Reduce wind drift losses
- Support a lower plunge basin or waterhole
- Help manage erosion if properly designed

The design should avoid concentrating flow in a way that scours banks or damages spillway areas.

## Lower Plunge Basin Or Waterhole Concept

The lower basin could act as a controlled receiving point for the cascade. It may also improve amenity and create a more natural water movement system than a fountain.

Key design questions:

- Where should the lower basin sit?
- Is it connected to any natural waterway or spillway?
- What erosion protection is required?
- How is overflow handled?
- Is fencing, signage, or edge treatment needed for safety?

## Safety And Erosion-Control Purpose

The concept should support safe water movement and erosion control, not just visual appeal.

Design considerations:

- Stable banks
- Rock lining or other erosion protection
- Safe access and fall prevention
- Pump intake screening
- Electrical safety near water
- Overflow path during storms
- Avoiding undermining of dam walls, spillways, or tracks

## Risks And Approval Unknowns

- Actual lift height may differ from the 20 m target.
- Pipe length and friction losses may require a larger pump or more panels.
- Shading may reduce pump runtime.
- Electrical compliance for second-hand panels must be checked.
- Works near waterways, spillways, or dam structures may require approvals.
- A plunge basin may affect erosion, safety, or waterway rules.
- Pump intake and return flows may disturb sediment or banks.

## Data Still Required

- Measured lift height
- Estimated pipe run length
- Pipe diameter options
- Sunny panel location
- Shading assessment
- Pump sizing advice
- Dam edge and return route sketch
- Approval check for plunge basin, waterway, spillway, or earthworks impacts

## Recommendation

Proceed with concept planning for a solar-only dam recirculation pump using second-hand panels, targeting approximately 6,000 L/hour at around 20 m lift.

Prefer a creek/cascade return over a fountain, provided erosion control, safety, and approval risks can be managed.
