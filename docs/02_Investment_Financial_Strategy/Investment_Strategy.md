# Investment and Financial Strategy

## Purpose

This section compares energy infrastructure investment against alternative uses of capital, especially keeping cash in the home loan offset.

The key question is not only whether solar and batteries reduce bills, but whether the investment beats the risk-adjusted return from retaining cash.

## Comparison Framework

Compare:

- Cash purchase of solar and battery system
- Green finance or equipment finance
- Waiting and keeping cash in the home loan offset

## Offset Account Benchmark

Assume the offset return equals avoided home loan interest.

For example, if the home loan interest rate is 6%, then each dollar held in offset earns an effective pre-tax return equivalent to avoiding 6% interest, subject to personal tax and loan structure considerations.

## Scenarios To Model

### Cash Purchase

Cash purchase should be assessed against:

- Simple payback
- NPV
- IRR
- Lost offset benefit
- Depreciation or tax treatment
- Residual asset value
- Maintenance and replacement costs

### Green Finance

Green finance should be assessed against:

- Loan interest rate
- Loan term
- Monthly repayment
- Whether savings exceed repayments
- Whether finance preserves offset cash
- Fees, security, and early repayment conditions

### Waiting

Waiting should be assessed against:

- Continued electricity bills
- Potential equipment price changes
- Future tariff changes
- Battery incentive timing
- Risk of missing grants or incentives
- Benefit of better interval data before committing

## Financial Metrics

### Net Present Value

Placeholder: calculate NPV using expected annual savings, capital cost, maintenance costs, replacement costs, and discount rate.

### Internal Rate of Return

Placeholder: calculate IRR using initial investment and annual net cash flows.

### Simple Payback

Placeholder: capital cost divided by annual savings.

### Opportunity Cost

Placeholder: compare annual savings against the avoided home loan interest that would have been earned by keeping the same cash in offset.
