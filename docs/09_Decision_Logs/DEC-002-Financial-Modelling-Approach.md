# DEC-002: Financial Modelling Approach

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-04

## Decision

Use current $1,200/month electricity bill as the primary financial baseline, with 93 kWh/day as the supporting usage baseline. Financial decisions must compare solar against the opportunity cost of leaving capital in the loan offset.

## Context

The site has a known average electricity bill of approximately $1,200/month, equal to $14,400/year. Average usage is approximately 93 kWh/day, but the daily average does not show time-of-use patterns, demand peaks, export constraints, or workshop load behaviour.

The Stage 1 energy project is being assessed as 50 kW solar + 100 kWh battery, designed for future expansion to 100 kW solar.

## Rationale

- The bill is the best current financial baseline because it captures tariff and usage effects.
- The 93 kWh/day usage figure remains useful for sizing and sanity checks.
- Capital used for solar has an opportunity cost if it would otherwise sit in the home loan offset.
- Finance should be assessed against both annual energy savings and preserved offset benefit.

## Consequences

- Every investment case must compare cash purchase against offset return.
- Finance options must be compared against the offset benchmark and not only against solar savings.
- Interval data remains a high-priority input before final approval.
- NPV and IRR should remain placeholders until quotes, tax treatment, maintenance, and replacement assumptions are known.
