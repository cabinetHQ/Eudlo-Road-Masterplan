# DEC-003: Stay Grid-Connected Initially

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-04

## Decision

Design the system to be microgrid-capable, but remain grid-connected initially until real-world solar, battery and workshop load data proves off-grid operation is safe and financially sensible.

## Context

The Stage 1 energy project is being planned around 50 kW solar + 100 kWh battery, with infrastructure designed for future 100 kW solar expansion.

The property has current workshop loads and may later add dam pumping, EV charging, tiny home manufacturing, future buildings, irrigation, and accommodation. The current interval load profile, demand charges, export limits, and machine start-up loads are not yet known.

## Rationale

- Grid connection reduces operational risk while data is collected.
- Off-grid operation may create generator dependency.
- Workshop downtime may be more costly than grid supply charges.
- Future load growth is uncertain.
- Microgrid-capable design keeps future options open.

## Consequences

- Quotes should include grid-connected operation and backup/microgrid capability.
- Off-grid claims should be treated cautiously until supported by data.
- Generator integration should support resilience, not become the primary energy strategy.
- A later disconnection decision should be made only after measured performance data is available.
