# DEC-005: Separate Solar And Efficiency Grants

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-04

## Decision

Treat solar/battery incentives separately from workshop energy-efficiency grants. Do not rely on MEEG for solar or batteries.

## Context

Project Eudlo is assessing a Stage 1 energy system of 50 kW solar + 100 kWh battery, with future expansion to 100 kW solar. It is also assessing workshop energy-efficiency opportunities such as compressor upgrades, variable speed drives, LED lighting, efficient motors, energy monitoring, and automatic shut-off controls.

Queensland MEEG guidance excludes energy generation equipment such as solar panels and electrical energy storage equipment such as batteries. It may still support eligible manufacturing energy-efficiency upgrades.

## Rationale

- Solar and battery incentives have separate federal pathways.
- MEEG is intended for energy-efficiency improvements, not generation or storage.
- Mixing ineligible solar/battery expenditure into an efficiency grant could weaken the grant strategy.
- Separating the streams makes the business case clearer for the accountant, bank, grant advisor, and future app ingestion.

## Consequences

- Solar quotes must show STCs and battery incentives separately.
- Workshop efficiency projects should have their own grant and payback analysis.
- MEEG should be investigated for compressor, VSD, lighting, monitoring, motors, and controls only.
- Finance strategy should separately compare solar/battery finance, green loans, and workshop equipment finance.
