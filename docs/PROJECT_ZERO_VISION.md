# Project Zero Vision

## Founder Vision: 2035

By 2035, Project Zero has become the operating system for the Eudlo Road property.

It is not a farm management app. It is not an asset register. It is not a project management tool. It is the live operating layer for a privately owned rural property that thinks, records, learns, warns, forecasts, and improves every year.

Project Zero began as the Eudlo App, but it became something larger: the proving ground for every serious idea about property, energy, manufacturing, water, ecology, infrastructure, finance, automation, and AI. BoundHQ may adopt ideas from it, but Project Zero is never constrained by BoundHQ.

## What It Has Become

Project Zero now knows the property as a system.

It knows every building, machine, road, fence, pipe, conduit, pump, switchboard, panel, battery, tree block, waterway, camera, sensor, document, quote, supplier, decision, grant, loan, maintenance event, and dollar invested.

It is the place where every future action is tested before it becomes real work.

## The Property Interface

The main screen is a live property map.

From that map, Brendan can see:

- Current power generation and battery state
- Workshop load and production activity
- Dam level, pump status, irrigation zones, and rainfall history
- Roads, tracks, gates, fences, and drainage
- Forestry blocks, habitat zones, and koala corridor areas
- Buildings, sheds, carports, and future building footprints
- Machinery status and maintenance risk
- Cameras, communications, and sensor health
- Active projects, decisions, and open actions by location

Nothing important is buried in folders.

## Decision Intelligence

Project Zero exists to eliminate poor decision making.

By 2035, every major idea is scored before money is spent. The system compares ROI, cashflow, risk, dependencies, property value, environmental benefit, grant eligibility, maintenance impact, complexity, and urgency.

It does not say yes to ideas because they sound exciting. It asks:

- What problem does this solve?
- What work does it remove later?
- What does it cost now?
- What does it cost if delayed?
- What breaks if it is wrong?
- What data is missing?
- What decision record will this create?

## Financial Intelligence

Every dollar is traceable.

Project Zero links capital spend to assets, projects, grants, suppliers, depreciation, insurance, maintenance, financing, savings, and opportunity cost.

The system can answer:

- What has been invested in the property?
- Which investments paid back?
- Which assets are under-maintained?
- Which assets are under-insured?
- Which projects are blocked by cashflow?
- Which upgrades improve business capacity?
- Which ideas should not be funded?

## Engineering Intelligence

Project Zero knows how the property is physically connected.

It understands:

- Electrical topology
- Water and irrigation topology
- Underground conduit routes
- Stormwater and drainage
- Communications coverage
- Building dependencies
- Machinery load profiles
- Generator and microgrid capability
- Future expansion pathways

Before anyone digs, wires, builds, buys, or installs, Project Zero shows the consequences.

## Ecological Intelligence

The system treats the land as part of the operating model.

Forestry, water, wildlife, erosion, koala corridor planning, fire risk, habitat, access tracks, and storm events are tracked alongside financial and engineering decisions.

The property improves because ecological decisions are not separate from infrastructure decisions.

## AI As A Property Chief Of Staff

Project Zero's AI is not a novelty layer. It is a chief of staff for the property.

It can:

- Read bills, quotes, photos, drone imagery, drawings, supplier emails, and maintenance logs
- Detect missing data
- Draft decision records
- Compare quotes
- Flag weak assumptions
- Suggest sequencing
- Predict maintenance needs
- Identify grant opportunities
- Warn when a project conflicts with previous decisions
- Explain why an idea should wait

The AI does not replace judgment. It protects judgment from forgetfulness, optimism, fragmentation, and duplicated work.

## The Operating System Principle

The final vision is simple:

Everything lives here.

Every asset has identity.
Every dollar has context.
Every decision has history.
Every project has outcomes.
Every system is expandable.
Every improvement reduces future work.
