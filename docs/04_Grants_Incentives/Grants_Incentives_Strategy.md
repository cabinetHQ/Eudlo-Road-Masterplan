# Grants, Incentives and Finance Strategy

## Executive Summary

Treat the Stage 1 solar/battery project and workshop energy-efficiency upgrades as separate funding streams.

Solar and batteries should be assessed through federal STCs, the federal battery incentive, supplier pricing, and green finance. Workshop efficiency projects should be assessed through MEEG-style grants, energy-efficiency grants, equipment finance, and tax treatment.

Do not rely on the Queensland MEEG grant for solar panels or batteries. MEEG may still be useful for compressor upgrades, variable speed drives, LED lighting, energy monitoring, efficient motors, and automatic shut-off controls.

## Federal Solar STCs

The Clean Energy Regulator explains that Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) are Australian Government incentives for eligible small-scale renewable energy systems. Individuals or businesses may earn STCs when eligible systems are installed.

For Project Eudlo:

- Ask each solar supplier to show the gross system price.
- Ask each supplier to show the STC discount separately.
- Confirm whether the proposed 50 kW Stage 1 system qualifies under the relevant rules.
- Confirm how any future expansion to 100 kW affects certificate treatment.
- Keep the STC assumptions visible for the accountant and bank.

Source: [Clean Energy Regulator - Small-scale technology certificates](https://www.cleanenergyregulator.gov.au/RET/Pages/Scheme%20participants%20and%20industry/Agents%20and%20installers/Small-scale%20technology%20certificates/Small-scale-technology-certificates.aspx)

## Federal Battery Incentive

The Cheaper Home Batteries Program provides a discount for eligible small-scale battery systems. Current official guidance says the program supports residential and non-residential premises, battery installations between 5 kWh and 100 kWh nominal capacity, and STCs for the first 50 kWh of useable capacity.

For Project Eudlo:

- Confirm whether the proposed 100 kWh Stage 1 battery qualifies.
- Confirm the discount treatment for the first 50 kWh of useable capacity.
- Confirm approved battery and inverter requirements.
- Ask suppliers to separate battery incentive value from solar STCs.
- Ask suppliers to show the post-incentive net battery cost.

Sources:

- [DCCEEW - Cheaper Home Batteries Program](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/energy/programs/cheaper-home-batteries)
- [DCCEEW - Eligibility information](https://www.dcceew.gov.au/energy/programs/cheaper-home-batteries/eligibility-information)

## Queensland MEEG Grant

The Queensland Manufacturing Energy Efficiency Grant Program should be investigated for workshop energy-efficiency projects, not for solar generation or battery storage.

The Round 2 MEEG guidelines list exclusions including energy generation equipment such as solar panels, electrical energy storage equipment such as batteries, second-hand equipment, and complete building rewiring or switchboard upgrades.

Source: [Queensland MEEG Round 2 guidelines PDF](https://www.rdmw.qld.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0007/1911193/meeg-guidelines-round-2.pdf)

## Why MEEG Excludes Solar And Batteries

MEEG is an energy-efficiency grant, not a general renewable generation grant. The policy logic is that it funds upgrades that reduce energy use in manufacturing operations, rather than equipment that generates or stores electricity.

For Project Eudlo, this means:

- Do not include solar panels in a MEEG application.
- Do not include batteries in a MEEG application.
- Do not rely on MEEG for switchboard upgrades required for solar/battery installation.
- Keep the solar business case separate from the workshop efficiency grant case.

## How MEEG Could Still Help

MEEG may be useful for workshop upgrades that reduce electricity consumption or demand.

Possible candidates:

- Compressor upgrade
- Variable speed drives
- Efficient motors
- LED lighting
- Energy monitoring
- Automatic shut-off controls
- Process controls that reduce idle equipment time

Each candidate should be supported by:

- Current equipment details
- Energy use baseline
- Proposed equipment specification
- Supplier quote
- Estimated kWh or demand reduction
- Payback estimate
- Evidence that the project is not solar, battery, second-hand equipment, or switchboard upgrade work

## ARENA Advancing Renewables Suitability Assessment

ARENA's Advancing Renewables Program supports development, demonstration, and pre-commercial deployment projects. It is unlikely to suit an ordinary commercial solar/battery installation at Eudlo Road.

ARENA may only become relevant if the project is reframed as a genuine demonstration or innovation project with broader learning value, such as a replicable manufacturing microgrid, advanced load optimisation, or novel integration of production scheduling and energy control.

Current assessment:

| Use case | Suitability |
| --- | --- |
| Ordinary 50 kW solar + 100 kWh battery install | Low |
| Standard workshop energy efficiency | Low |
| Demonstration microgrid with publishable industry learnings | Possible but unproven |
| AI-driven manufacturing and energy optimisation demonstration | Possible but requires advisor review |

Source: [ARENA - Advancing Renewables Program](https://arena.gov.au/funding/advancing-renewables-program/)

## CEFC-Backed Green Finance

The Clean Energy Finance Corporation describes itself as Australia's specialist climate investor and supports finance for clean energy, energy efficiency, and emissions reduction. CEFC-backed products may be offered through participating lenders rather than directly to Project Eudlo.

For Project Eudlo:

- Ask banks and brokers whether any CEFC-backed green finance is available.
- Confirm whether solar, batteries, energy monitoring, compressor upgrades, VSDs, and efficient motors are eligible.
- Compare CEFC-backed finance against home loan offset opportunity cost.
- Confirm rate, term, fees, security, early repayment, and tax treatment.

Source: [CEFC](https://www.cefc.com.au/)

## Bank Green Loans

Bank green loans may be useful if they preserve cash in the offset account and the repayments are covered by electricity savings.

Ask each bank:

- Is the product available for a business, mixed-use property, or manufacturing site?
- Does it cover solar?
- Does it cover batteries?
- Does it cover energy-efficiency equipment?
- What is the comparison rate?
- What fees apply?
- What security is required?
- Can the loan be repaid early?
- Can the loan fund a staged 50 kW now, 100 kW later strategy?

## Accountant And Tax Actions

Ask the accountant to advise on:

- Depreciation treatment for solar, battery, inverter, switchboard, monitoring, and generator integration
- GST treatment
- Business/private use apportionment
- Whether finance interest is deductible
- Whether any instant asset write-off or temporary full-expensing style rules apply at the time of purchase
- Whether workshop efficiency upgrades should be separated from the solar/battery investment case
- Whether any BoundHQ or cabinetHQ development work may qualify for the R&D Tax Incentive

## Working Recommendation

Use four separate tracks:

| Track | Purpose | Current position |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Solar STCs | Reduce solar upfront cost | Confirm through supplier quotes |
| Battery incentive | Reduce battery upfront cost | Confirm eligibility and approved equipment |
| MEEG or efficiency grants | Fund workshop efficiency upgrades only | Investigate compressor, VSD, LED, monitoring, controls |
| Green finance and tax | Improve cash flow and offset comparison | Ask accountant, bank, broker, and R&D advisor |

Keep solar/battery incentives separate from workshop energy-efficiency grants so the business case remains clean and grant applications do not rely on ineligible expenditure.
