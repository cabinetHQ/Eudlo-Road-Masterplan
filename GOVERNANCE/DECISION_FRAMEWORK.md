# Decision Framework

## Purpose

This framework provides a common scoring system for future ideas, purchases, projects, and infrastructure changes.

Scores are from 1 to 10, where 1 is weak or unfavourable and 10 is strong or favourable.

## Scoring Criteria

| Criterion | Score 1 Means | Score 10 Means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Financial ROI | Poor or unclear return | Strong measurable return |
| Cashflow | Large negative cashflow | Positive or manageable cashflow |
| Risk reduction | Adds risk | Materially reduces risk |
| Business growth | No growth impact | Strong business growth enabler |
| Property value | No value impact | Strong property value improvement |
| Future flexibility | Locks in options | Keeps many future options open |
| Environmental benefit | No benefit or negative impact | Strong environmental benefit |
| Grant eligibility | No likely grant support | Strong confirmed grant alignment |
| Maintenance impact | Adds maintenance burden | Reduces maintenance or improves reliability |
| Complexity | Very complex | Simple and easy to execute |
| Urgency | Can wait | Time-critical or blocking |
| Dependencies | Many unresolved dependencies | Few dependencies or dependencies resolved |
| Overall Priority | Low priority | Highest priority |

## Guidance

- Use the scorecard for capital purchases, infrastructure projects, grant candidates, app features, and major operational changes.
- Do not treat the total score as automatic approval.
- Use the score to expose trade-offs and force clear thinking.
- If a criterion is unknown, mark it `TBD` and add an action item.
- Any accepted major decision still needs a decision record.
